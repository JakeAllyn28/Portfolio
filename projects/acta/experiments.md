# Experimental Systems & Instrumentation

**Context:** ACTA-SH
**Focus:** Experimental validation, instrumentation, physics model verification

---

## Overview

Designed and executed full-scale and controlled experiments to validate physics-based simulation models of blast effects, debris propagation, and structural response.

These experiments directly informed and calibrated fast-running simulation models used for large-scale risk analysis and government applications .

---

## Blast Testing Program

### Overview

Conducted three full-scale blast tests to measure how structural elements and internal infrastructure respond to explosive loading.

Each test environment included realistic building infrastructure to capture secondary effects such as debris generation and internal damage propagation.

---

## Experimental Setup

Each blast test included:

* Multiple wall types (e.g., CMU, reinforced concrete)
* Internal infrastructure:

  * HVAC system
  * Water heater
  * Electrical transformers
  * Instrumented door system
* Two impulse sled systems for debris impact measurement

---

## Instrumentation

Designed and deployed a multi-modal instrumentation setup:

* High-speed cameras for debris tracking
* Accelerometers for structural and object response
* Pressure gauges for blast characterization
* LiDAR scans for post-test spatial reconstruction
* GoPro cameras for additional coverage
* Custom GPS + accelerometer system for door response tracking

---

## Data Collection & Processing

* Manually collected and cataloged debris post-test
* Correlated debris mass, velocity, and impact behavior
* Captured impulse data via sled "witness plates"
* Generated datasets for validating simulation outputs

Developed tools and workflows to process experimental data into formats usable for simulation comparison and calibration.

---

## AR-Based Debris Scanning

Developed a mobile AR application to:

* Scan debris fields in real time
* Generate spatially accurate point clouds
* Provide a low-cost alternative to traditional scanning methods

This enabled faster and more flexible post-test data collection.

---

## Purpose & Impact

* Validated fast-running simulation models of structural response and debris behavior
* Provided ground truth data for Monte Carlo risk analysis pipelines
* Improved confidence in simulation outputs used for safety and government applications

---

## Drop Tower System

### Overview

Designed and built an in-house drop tower system to perform controlled impact experiments.

This allowed for repeatable testing of debris impacts and material response under known conditions.

---

## Use Cases

### Debris Impact Correlation

* Used debris collected from blast tests as impactors
* Measured impulse on sled witness plates
* Correlated controlled impact data with full-scale blast results

---

### Material Penetration Studies (FAA)

* Conducted controlled impact testing on various materials
* Evaluated penetration thresholds and structural response
* Supported FAA-related studies on material strength and failure behavior

---

## Technical Contributions

* Integrated multiple sensor modalities into unified datasets
* Developed repeatable testing methodologies
* Bridged experimental results with simulation model validation
